# Equilibrium with Endogenous Pool Return: Proof Notes

*Extension of the insurance paper's Theorem 1 to a pool return that declines
toward the market rate as capital crowds in. Analytical statements here;
computational verification in `prove_equilibrium.py` (VERDICT output).*

## Setup

Replace the constant pool return with scarce alpha:

```
r_pool(C) = r_market + (r_pool0 - r_market) * K / (K + C),        (E1)
R(C)      = r_pool(C) * C     (total annual pool revenue),
```

where `C = C_LP + sum_i CC_i (+ C_S)` is total pool capital and `K > 0` the
alpha capacity. Protocol i's yield income becomes

```
Y_i(CC_i; CC_-i, C_LP) = (1-gamma)(1-phi) * R(C_-i + CC_i) * w_i,
w_i = CC_i / (S_-i + CC_i),      S_-i = sum_{j != i} CC_j,        (E2)
```

with `C_-i = C_LP + S_-i`. Everything else (coverage Eq. 2, utilization
Eq. 3, cap Eq. 5, yield share Eq. 8) is unchanged from the paper.

## Solution concept (restated after the adversarial audit, 2026-07-18)

Protocols are **aggregate-takers**: each optimizes taking the mechanism
aggregates — the yield share `gamma`, pool revenue `gross_rev`, prudential
scaling `cap_scale`, and the hazard index — as given, analogous to
price-taking in competitive equilibrium. Equilibrium additionally requires
that the aggregates are the ones generated by the chosen profile. The
implemented best response and the epsilon certificate are exact for THIS
game. In the full game, a deviating protocol also moves the aggregates;
the distance between the two concepts is MEASURED, not assumed:
recomputing all aggregates at the deviated profile, the largest-collateral
protocols gain at most ~1e-4 $M (~$100) per quarter (THEOREM1_FULLGAME in
`prove_equilibrium.py`). With heavy-tailed TVL (Pareto 1.2) this
aggregate-taking error is not uniformly O(1/n) — it is reported per run
rather than bounded analytically.

## Step 1 — Properties of R (symbolically verifiable)

**Fact 1.** Under (E1): R is continuous, strictly increasing, strictly
concave, with `R(0) = 0` and `R(C)/C -> r_market` as `C -> inf`.

*Proof.* `R(C) = r_market*C + s0*K*C/(K+C)` with `s0 = r_pool0 - r_market > 0`.
The first term is linear; the second has second derivative
`-2*s0*K^2/(K+C)^3 < 0`. QED (SymPy check in `prove_equilibrium.py`).

Any alternative decay satisfying Fact 1 works; nothing below uses the
hyperbolic form beyond these properties.

## Step 2 — The key lemma (own-payoff concavity of the yield term)

The obstacle to existence is that (E2) is a *product* of two terms moving
with own collateral. The lemma shows the aggregate-revenue concavity is
enough:

**Lemma 1.** If R is nondecreasing, concave, and `R(0) = 0`, then
`f(c) = R(C_LP + S_-i + c) * c/(S_-i + c)` is concave in `c >= 0`.

*Proof.* Write `A = C_LP + S_-i >= S = S_-i`, `w(c) = c/(S+c)`. Then
`f'' = R''w + 2R'w' + Rw''` with `w' = S/(S+c)^2 > 0`,
`w'' = -2S/(S+c)^3 < 0`. Concavity of R through the origin gives the
average-exceeds-marginal property `R'(A+c) <= R(A+c)/(A+c) <= R(A+c)/(S+c)`.
Hence

```
2R'w' <= 2 * [R/(S+c)] * S/(S+c)^2 = -R w'',
```

so `f'' <= R'' w <= 0`. QED.

Intuition: a protocol adding collateral gains pool share (concave: `w'' < 0`)
but dilutes the pool's per-dollar alpha (`R' <= R/C`); the dilution
dominates the convexifying cross term. The lemma also covers the constant
`r_pool` case (R linear: `R' = R/C`, inequalities weak), so existence was
never the issue in the fixed-alpha game — the carry trade was a statement
about *levels* (unbounded aggregate demand up to the wealth cap). Strictly
concave R additionally caps the aggregate (Step 4).

**Symbolic proof (hyperbolic R) — achieved by the adversarial audit.**
Substituting `A = S + d` (d >= 0), `f''` reduces to a rational function
whose denominator factors into positive terms `(S+c)^3 (K+S+c+d)^3` and
whose expanded numerator is a polynomial in `(c, S, d, K, s0, r_m)` with
ALL coefficients nonpositive — hence `f'' <= 0` identically on the
admissible domain. Reproduced in `prove_equilibrium.py` (LEMMA1, exact).
Caveats: general concave R needs a C^2 mollification step (routine); and
under the code convention `w(0) = 0` the map f jumps upward at `c = 0+`
when `S = 0` — f remains concave on `[0, inf)` and `_golden_cc` probes the
`C_C = 0` endpoint explicitly, so the optimizer is unaffected.

**Where Lemma 1 binds.** In the implemented (aggregate-taking) best
response, `gross_rev` is held fixed, so the yield term's concavity in own
`C_C` follows from `w'' < 0` alone; Lemma 1 is the stronger statement for
the game in which the deviator's collateral moves `R(C)` itself, showing
concavity survives that internalization. Concavity through the remaining
full-game channels — own `C_C` moving `gamma` (via the fair split) and
`cap_scale` (via utilization) — is NOT established; this is why the
full-game deviation check uses a dense grid scan rather than a local
optimizer, and why Theorem 1' below is stated for the aggregate-taking
game.

## Step 3 — Existence (Theorem 1', mirroring the paper's Theorem 1)

**Theorem 1'.** Consider the AGGREGATE-TAKING stage game with strategy
sets `CC_i in [0, W_i]`, `h_i in [0, h_max]`, `C_LP in [0, W_LP]`,
speculators competitive risk-neutral (prices = induced attack
probabilities), pool return (E1), and protocols optimizing against the
aggregates generated by the profile. If, for fixed rival strategies and
aggregates, each protocol payoff is quasi-concave in own `(CC_i, h_i)`
and the LP payoff quasi-concave in `C_LP` (A1'), then an equilibrium
(profile + self-consistent aggregates) exists.

*Proof sketch.* Strategy sets are nonempty, compact, convex. Payoffs are
continuous: `r_pool(.)` is continuous (E1), coverage, gamma (clipped),
and the attacker best-response objects `p_q(V, h)`, `b*(V, h)` are
continuous in `h` by the maximum theorem (the attacker's problem has
continuous objective and compact effort domain, and its solution enters
protocol payoffs only through continuous functionals). Under (A1'),
best-response correspondences are nonempty, convex-valued, u.h.c. (Berge);
Kakutani on the product delivers a fixed point, exactly as in the paper's
Appendix C. QED.

**Discharging (A1') instead of assuming it.** In the aggregate-taking
game (`gamma`, `gross_rev`, `cap_scale` fixed during optimization — the
solution concept, not a hidden assumption), the protocol payoff in own
collateral is a sum of: (i) expected-payout term — concave in CC
(composition of the concave coverage `mu*CC^theta`, theta < 1, with the
concave nondecreasing `E[min(., L)]`); (ii) forfeiture and opportunity
cost — linear; (iii) the yield term — concave since `w'' < 0` (and by
**Lemma 1** even if the deviator's effect on `R` is internalized). Sums
of concave functions are concave, so the payoff is concave (hence
quasi-concave) in `CC_i` for every `h_i` — *no weak-feedback assumption
needed for the collateral margin*, an improvement over assuming (A1)
wholesale. (Full-game quasi-concavity through the `gamma`/`cap_scale`
channels remains open; the full-game deviation gap is measured instead.) The `h`-margin (through `p_q(h)`, `b*(h)`, `xi(h)`) is not
globally concave in general; in the implementation `h` lives on a finite
grid, so the existence argument applies to the finite-action mixed
extension (Nash 1951) or, in practice, we verify ex post that the computed
pure profile admits no profitable unilateral deviation on the full grid
(`deviation_check`, VERDICT in `prove_equilibrium.py`).

Note on the aggregate: given the equilibrium yield share `gamma` and cap
scale, the collateral subgame is an *aggregative game* (payoffs depend on
own CC and the aggregate S). With strictly concave own-payoffs, best
responses are single-valued and continuous, so the aggregate best-response
map `S -> sum_i BR_i(S)` is continuous on `[0, sum_i W_i]` and Brouwer
gives the fixed point directly — this is the computational route the code's
damped iteration follows.

## Step 4 — Carry-trade extinction (the economics of the extension)

**Proposition E2 (corrected after the adversarial audit).** Let
`rbar(C) = [R(C) + F(C)] / C` be the all-in per-dollar pool revenue rate
(base return plus speculator-fee inflows; in the implementation
`rbar -> r_market + fee_base` as `C -> inf`, NOT `r_market`). At any
interior equilibrium with small own price impact, the marginal annual
value of collateral satisfies

```
(1-phi) * (1-gamma*) * (C*/S*) * rbar(C*) + MB_cov,i
      =  r_market + 4*p_i + o(1),                                  (E3')
```

where `(1-gamma*) C*/S*` is the protocols' revenue share per dollar of
posted collateral, `MB_cov,i >= 0` the marginal coverage/risk-utility
benefit, and `4*p_i` the annualized forfeiture premium. The earlier draft
substituted `(1-gamma*) ~ S*/C*` (pure fair split), which the audit
refuted quantitatively: with the paper's App.-B blend (`eta_blend = 0.5`)
and `gamma_raw >> gamma_fair`, the measured factor is ~0.6, and the fee
wedge is first-order. Hence:

1. The pure carry motive dies where `(1-phi)(1-gamma*)(C*/S*) rbar(C*)`
   falls to `r_market + 4*p_i - MB_cov,i` — an implicit bound on `C*`;
   with fees, per-dollar revenue does not decay all the way to
   `r_market`, and in the present calibration a majority of protocols sit
   at the wealth cap `cc_tvl_cap * TVL`, so their collateral is pinned by
   wealth and forfeiture risk rather than alpha decay.
2. `C*` is nondecreasing in capacity K (numerical comparative static,
   verified in the K sweep), and as `K -> inf` the fixed-alpha carry
   regime re-emerges.
3. (E3') is testable in the simulation: at K = 500 the measured marginal
   yield (0.0549/yr) matches the decomposition
   `(1-phi)(1-gamma*)(C*/S*) rbar(C*)` (0.0553/yr) to within 1%.

*Proof sketch.* Differentiate the protocol payoff in `CC_i` at an
interior optimum; the aggregate-taking marginal yield per collateral
dollar is `(1-gamma*)(1-phi) * gross_rev / S*` (own price impact enters
at `O(c_i/C)` by Lemma 1's average/marginal bound); equate to marginal
cost. QED (numerical checks: K sweep + the E3' decomposition above).

## Computation: the four-phase stage solver and its certificate

`solve_stage` (game.py) computes each quarter's equilibrium in four phases:
1. damped simultaneous best-response iteration (global stability);
2. undamped polish on self-consistent aggregates, keeping the profile with
   the smallest certified epsilon;
3. sequential (Gauss-Seidel) refinement over protocols with positive gain —
   breaks the 2-cycles that simultaneous best response produces among
   near-indifferent protocols;
4. exhaustive enumeration over small flip sets (<= 5 protocols) — finds an
   exact pure profile among the cycling candidates if one exists.

The reported `eps_stage` is the maximum unilateral (h, C_C) utility gain at
the accepted profile under its own aggregates — the Nash criterion itself,
with the C_C dimension exact by Lemma 1 (concave => golden section exact).
Empirically ~90% of epochs certify at eps < 1e-9 $M; the remainder plateau
at eps ~ 1e-4 $M (a few hundred dollars per quarter) where phases 3-4 find
no exact profile — consistent with genuine non-existence of exact
pure-strategy equilibria in the finite-h game at those states.
